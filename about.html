About

Yongze Song is an Associate Professor at Curtin University. He is an Australian Young Tall Poppy Science Award (YTP) Winner, Royal Geographical Society (RGS) Fellow, UK, Harvard SDL Fellow, US, DAAD AInet Fellow, Germany, and a Committee Member of the International Association for Mathematical Geosciences (IAMG).

Yongze leads the Geospatial Intelligence Lab, maintaining strong industry networks and delivering industry-driven research projects about sustainable infrastructure management using emerging technologies. He developed more than 30 new geospatial methods and 12 R software packages with over 200,000 downloads for understanding geographical and spatial data. He published over 140 peer-reviewed journal articles, including 9 highly-cited papers. He is a recipient of more than ten awards at international and national levels, such as the Geospatial World 50 Rising Stars, the Global Top 10 Young Scientist Award, World's Top 2% Scientists by Stanford University, and the Australian National Location Data First Prize.

He is an Associate Editor of five international peer-reviewed journals, including Q1 journals of International Journal of Applied Earth Observation and Geoinformation, GIScience & Remote Sensing, and Geoscientific Model Development. He serves as an Editorial Board Member of 17 journals, a Guest Editor for 20 journal special issues, a Chair or Program Committee Member for over 30 international conferences, and a peer reviewer for over 80 journals. He was invited to present over 100 research seminars at universities (e.g., Harvard University, MIT, Yale, ANU, NUS, etc.) and industries globally.

Research Interests

Sustainable development, spatial statistics, geospatial theories, sustainable infrastructure, remote sensing, geospatial artificial intelligence, megaproject management
