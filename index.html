Yongze Song is a Lecturer at Curtin University, Australia. He is a Fellow of Royal Geographical Society (with IBG), UK, and Editorial Board member of "GIScience & Remote Sensing" (IF 5.965, Q1). He is a global top 1% reviewer in Geosciences (Publons), lead guest editor of “Remote Sensing” (IF 4.509, Q1), and lifetime member of International Association for Mathematical Geosciences (IAMG).

His research interests include spatial statistics, geospatial methods, urban remote sensing, sustainable infrastructure and sustainable development.

Yongze Song has published 35 peer-reviewed journal articles (ResearchGate), including 30 articles published on JCR Q1 journals, including IJGIS, RSE, IEEE T-GRS, RSER, IEEE T-ITS, IEEE T-GRSL, IEEE JSTARS, GRS, IJRS, JCP and EHJ. The articles include two ESI highly cited papers. His h-index is 15 (Google Scholar).

He has developed 7 geospatial data analysis models and methods and published 5 R open-source packages on CRAN for the innovative models, which has been downloaded over 56,000 times globally. The new methods and tools have supported industries to do large-scale data processing, analytics and decision making in sustainable infrastructure, asset management and intelligent transport in practical works.

He is a recipient of multiple awards at international, national and local levels, including the Australian National First Prize of Location Data, Chinese Government Award for Outstanding Self-Financed Students Abroad (top 0.1% globally), two Australian National Honourable Mention Awards of location data analysis, two international Honourable Mention Awards in international mathematical contests ICM and MCM, Chancellor's Commendation for Exceptionally High Standard in the PhD Thesis, Outstanding Graduate Award, China National Scholarship for Postgraduates (top 1%) and National Scholarship (top 1%).
